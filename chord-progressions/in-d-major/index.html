The pop axis

The four-chord engine behind a thousand pop hits. The lift from I to V opens the chorus, vi pulls down into feeling, IV walks back toward home.

A rich, unexpected major chord built on the flattened third. A favourite of indie and dream-pop writers.

Why songwriters reach for D major

The key signature of D major is two sharps (F#, C#). That gives you open D, G, A and Bm all available without barre chords; piano sits in a comfortable middle register. Practical implications: barre chords are unavoidable, but a capo can move the music into open-string territory. piano players find the hand position natural. The reason songwriters reach for D major again and again is the open-tuned guitar key for fiddle tunes, and they accept its quirks to land that colour: sea shanties, and a third of the folk repertoire.
