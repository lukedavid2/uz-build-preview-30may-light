Chord progressions in Bb major

Pick Bb major for a singalong moment. It is the brass-band home key, friendly to horns and saxes. Its tonic chord carries stability, and the rest of its diatonic chords arrange themselves around that gravity. Concert pitch for nearly every brass instrument, which is why so much funk and big-band sits here. The pop axis

The four-chord engine behind a thousand pop hits. The lift from I to V opens the chorus, vi pulls down into feeling, IV walks back toward home.

A rich, unexpected major chord built on the flattened third. A favourite of indie and dream-pop writers.

Why songwriters reach for Bb major

The key signature of Bb major is two flats (Bb, Eb). That gives you the home key of every brass instrument; tenor sax sits exactly here in concert pitch terms. Practical implications: the shapes fit well under common fingerings. the keyboard layout rewards steady scale practice. The reason songwriters reach for Bb major again and again is the brass band key, and they accept its quirks to land that colour: used by big bands, marching bands and jazz horn sections.
