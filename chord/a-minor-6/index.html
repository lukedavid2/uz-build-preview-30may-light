Am6 chord

Start with the recipe. The notes are A - C - E - F#, a minor sixth, otherwise known as the Am6. The chord feels a minor with a touch of brightness, which is why writers chasing a vintage minor flavour keep landing on it. Light My Fire by The Doors leans on Am6 throughout the verse. You will find it inside vintage jazz and 1930s-style ballads. The rest of this page lays out the common voicings, the interval theory, the progressions where the chord fits, and the records that lean on it for structural work.

The theory behind Am6

Spell out the Am6 and you get A - C - E - F#. The intervals from the root are root, minor third, perfect fifth, major sixth, which is the recipe for a minor sixth. In a closely related major key the chord plays the role of passing chord; in a closely related major key it shows up as passing chord. Its preferred next move is resolve to the dominant or back to the i, which is what makes it useful in the Great American Songbook.
