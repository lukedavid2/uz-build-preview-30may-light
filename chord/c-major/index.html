C chord

Think of the C as warm, settled and direct. The chord is built from C - E - G, a textbook major triad. It does its strongest work in campfire songs and stadium choruses, where it tends to anchor the chorus. The C shows up in more songs than you would expect. Most beginner songbooks open with C, but the chord stays useful long past the beginner stage. Voicings on both instruments, theory in plain language, progressions in multiple keys and a handful of real song references are all laid out below.

The theory behind C

C is a major triad built on C. Its three or four notes (C - E - G) sit a specific distance apart: root, major third, perfect fifth. That makes it a Tonic (I) in the key of C major, and the same chord works as the Subdominant (IV) in G major. The simplest rule of thumb: the C wants to stay home or move to the IV or V.
