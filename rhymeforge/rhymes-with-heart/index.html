Words that rhyme with Heart

Singers reaching for heart find a word the love songs all share on the surface and a one-syllable core on the /ɜːr/ that mid-Atlantic ears class as one vowel underneath — one that ends with a liquid-stop kick. Pop and folk have leaned on it for a century. When a songwriter asks what rhymes with heart, the pool tells a specific story: only a handful of strict perfect rhymes survive, nothing lands in the family-rhyme column, while the assonance options multiply into the thousands. Its lyric role is a word the listener can feel. Lean on assonance and the song will sound contemporary, not catalogued.

Why heart rhymes the way it does

Heart is one-syllable, its rhyme-relevant vowel sitting on the rhotic schwa, then it ends with a liquid-stop kick. In our 54,000-word dictionary the perfect-rhyme pool returns 41 matches, family rhymes 0, additive and subtractive together 135, assonance 7,686, and consonance 101. The empty family column matters less than it looks. Family rhymes are a strict-classifier construct; the songwriter's ear accepts most assonance matches in their place. The songwriter's move is to pick a small set of strict rhymes for the chorus and open up to family and assonance through the verses. Heart rewards slant rhyming because the strict pool, when over-used, calls attention to itself.
