Why ember rhymes the way it does

Ember is two-syllable, its rhyme-relevant vowel sitting on the /ɜːr/ that mid-Atlantic ears class as one vowel, then it spills out through a liquid consonant. In our 54,000-word dictionary the perfect-rhyme pool returns 9 matches, family rhymes 0, additive and subtractive together 29, assonance 11,481, and consonance 36. The empty family column matters less than it looks. Family rhymes are a strict-classifier construct; the songwriter's ear accepts most assonance matches in their place. Practical guidance: read the song aloud and listen for where a slant would tighten the line. Strict rhymes are the structural skeleton; the slant columns are where the personality of the lyric lives. With ember, the slant work is doing more weight-bearing than it looks.
