Why night rhymes the way it does

To understand why night rhymes the way it does, start with the vowel — the open /aɪ/ diphthong, written /aɪt/ — and the ending, which closes on a hard stop. In our 54,000-word dictionary the perfect-rhyme pool returns 138 matches, family rhymes 0, additive and subtractive together 128, assonance 2,921, and consonance 995. That's a deep perfect column. The song can ride strict rhymes from end to end, only sliding into assonance when the line needs to surprise. The songwriter's move is to pick a small set of strict rhymes for the chorus and open up to family and assonance through the verses. Night rewards slant rhyming because the strict pool, when over-used, calls attention to itself.
